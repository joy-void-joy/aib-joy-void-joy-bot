# CHP Extraordinary Congress Notice - Research Notes

## Key Timeline
- May 21: Court annulled CHP 38th Congress, reinstated Kılıçdaroğlu
- June 1: Özel camp began collecting delegate signatures
- June 2: Signature threshold exceeded (700+ vs ~550 needed)
- June 12: 111 of 138 MPs declared calling for extraordinary congress on July 12
- June 15: Both camps canceled parliamentary group meetings
- June 16 (today): 900 signatures collected, 15-day collection period ended
- June 17-18: Signatures to be submitted to CHP headquarters
- Deadline: Notice must be published by June 27 for question to resolve YES

## Two Possible Target Dates
1. **July 12** - declared by 111 MPs on June 12, aligned with July 20 election deadline
2. **September 21** - mentioned in Habertürk article (possibly current plan or earlier plan)

## Legal Process After Signature Submission
1. Signatures submitted to HQ
2. General Chairman (Kılıçdaroğlu) has authority to issue convening notice
3. If chairman refuses → apply to Sulh Hukuk Mahkemesi (Civil Court)
4. Court appoints "çağrı heyeti" (convening committee)
5. Committee issues formal notice

Alternative: Delegates may self-issue notice under CHP statute Article 48

## Key Legal Arguments
- **FOR congress**: Prof. Ersan Şen says current management cannot prevent delegate-initiated congress
- **AGAINST**: Hürriyet columnist Yalçın Bayer questions whether either side can effectively convene
- **Kılıçdaroğlu camp**: Interim injunction prevents any congress until ruling is final
- **Özel camp**: Injunction is no barrier; a new congress is a "new founding process"

## Resolution Criteria Analysis
- Qualifying notice can come from delegates acting under statute (not just party leadership)
- Notice counts even if later challenged, UNLESS court voids it within the window
- Political declarations/calls by MPs don't count
- Must be reported as "official" by Hürriyet Daily News or credible source
