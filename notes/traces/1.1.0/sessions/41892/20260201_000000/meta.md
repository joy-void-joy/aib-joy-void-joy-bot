# Meta-Reflection

## Executive Summary
Post ID: 41892 - "Will the Liberal Democratic Party win a majority in the 2026 Japanese general election?"
Final forecast: ~60% (0.60)

The question is about whether the LDP alone (not the coalition) will win 233+ seats in the February 8, 2026 snap election called by PM Takaichi. Major newspaper seat projections (Yomiuri, Nikkei Jan 27-28) indicate LDP is on track to exceed 233 alone, with "stable majority" (243) "within range." However, 2024 polling overestimate precedent and the unprecedented Komeito defection to the opposition create significant downside risk.

## Evidence Assessment

*Strongest evidence FOR (LDP gets majority):*
1. Yomiuri/Nikkei seat projections (Jan 27-28) specifically indicate LDP on track to exceed 233 alone - these are credible district-by-district analyses
2. Takaichi's ~70% cabinet approval is exceptionally high and much stronger than Ishiba's approval before the 2024 election
3. LDP vote share in most polls at 35-42% is strong for Japan's fragmented party system

*Strongest argument AGAINST:*
- In 2024, similar pre-election polling overestimated LDP by ~20+ seats. If that happens again, 240→220 = no majority
- Komeito's defection to opposition (CDP+Komeito alliance) is historically unprecedented and could cost LDP in competitive single-member districts where Komeito's organized voters (Soka Gakkai) provided crucial support
- Some polls (Mainichi) show LDP as low as 27% support
- Professor quoted: "it's not clear if high public support for the Takaichi cabinet will actually lead to support of the LDP"
