# SCOTUS Granted & Noted List Analysis

## Key Finding: The OT 2025 list and OT 2026 list are SEPARATE

Cases are placed on the list for the TERM in which they will be ARGUED, not the term during which cert was granted.

### Evidence:
- **OT 2025 list** (25grantednotedlist.pdf, as of March 4, 2026): Latest grants are from 1/16/26 (Hikma, Monsanto, Chatrie). No grants after January 2026.
- **OT 2026 list** (26grantednotedlist.pdf, as of March 9, 2026): Already has 4 cases:
  - Anderson v. Intel (Granted 1/16/26) - on OT 2026 list, NOT OT 2025
  - Salazar v. Paramount Global (Granted 1/26/26) - on OT 2026 list
  - Suncor Energy v. Boulder Cty (Granted 2/23/26) - on OT 2026 list
  - Dept. of Air Force v. Guahan (Granted 3/9/26) - on OT 2026 list

### Critical Implication:
The March 9, 2026 cert grant (Guahan) appears on the OT 2026 list, NOT the OT 2025 list. This confirms that March-April 2026 grants go on the OT 2026 list.

### For the question to resolve YES:
5+ cases would need to be granted cert AND scheduled for argument during the remaining OT 2025 sessions (through late April 2026) with grant dates from March 5-April 30, 2026. This would require unprecedented fast-tracking.

### Historical base rate:
- OT 2024 list: Latest cert grant was 1/27/25. Zero cases granted March-April 2025 on OT 2024 list.
- OT 2025 list: Latest cert grant is 1/16/26. Zero cases granted March-April 2026 so far.
- Emergency stay applications (Trump v. CASA) appear as "STAY" not cert grants - excluded by fine print.

### Conclusion:
The probability is extremely low (~2-3%). The question appears predicated on a misunderstanding of list structure.
