# Meta Reflection: Nigeria Bank Account Penetration (Q10220)

## Executive Summary
- **Post ID**: 10220
- **Question**: Will 95% or more of Nigerians have a bank account before 2025?
- **Final Forecast**: ~1% (essentially NO)
- **Confidence**: Very high (95%+)

This is a **definitional question** - the deadline (January 1, 2025) has already passed. The task is to determine whether 95% was achieved, not to predict the future.

## Key Findings

### Financial Inclusion Data (2023 A2F Survey - Most Recent)
- **Bank account ownership**: 52% of adult Nigerians
- **Formal financial inclusion** (banks + non-bank channels): 64%
- **Overall financial inclusion** (any formal or informal): 74%
- **Financial exclusion**: 26% (28.8 million adults)

### Target vs Reality
- **2019 Target**: Governor Emefiele set goal of 95% by 2024
- **Actual Achievement**: ~52% bank accounts, ~64% formal inclusion
- **Gap**: 31-43 percentage points short of target

### Historical Trajectory
- 2011: 29.7%
- 2017: 39.7%
- 2023: 52%

Even the optimistic interpretation (74% overall inclusion) is far below 95%.
