# Meta Reflection: WHO H5N1 PHEIC Declaration (Q41908)

## Executive Summary
- **Question ID**: 41908
- **Title**: Will the WHO Director-General declare a PHEIC for H5N1 before May 1, 2026?
- **Final Forecast**: ~6% probability (low likelihood)
- **Confidence**: Moderate-high

The question asks whether WHO will declare a Public Health Emergency of International Concern for H5N1 between January 8, 2026 and May 1, 2026. My research indicates no PHEIC is currently imminent - there's no emergency committee meeting announced, no evidence of sustained human-to-human transmission, and WHO continues to assess risk as low.

## Research Process

### Strategy
1. Fetched the Metaculus question details
2. Searched for recent H5N1 news and WHO statements
3. Checked related Metaculus questions (especially Q30763 which resolved NO)
4. Looked for prediction market signals
5. Researched PHEIC criteria and historical precedents

### Most Valuable Sources
- WHO weekly avian influenza updates (January 2026)
- CDC H5 Bird Flu situation summary
- Wikipedia article on PHEIC (clarified automatic triggers)
- Metaculus Q30763 resolution (H5 PHEIC before 2026 = NO at 0.1%)
- Joint FAO/WHO/WOAH risk assessment

### Key Findings
1. No PHEIC declared for H5N1 as of Feb 2, 2026
2. WHO risk assessment: "low" for general population
3. No sustained human-to-human transmission detected
4. 71 US human cases since April 2024 (sporadic, occupational)
5. CDC ended emergency declaration in July 2025
6. New subtypes of HUMAN influenza are automatic PHEICs - but H5N1 is avian influenza
