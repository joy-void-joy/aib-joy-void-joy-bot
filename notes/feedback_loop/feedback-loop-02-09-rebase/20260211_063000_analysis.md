### Root Cause: Threshold-Crossing Guidance Backfired

Deep trace analysis of the three worse questions reveals:

**39935 (Mamdani, 0.50→0.47, YES):** The re-retrodiction generated three new bearish factors not in the original: "ceiling effect" (-0.21 logit), "polls already priced in" (-0.12), "status quo = NO" (-0.08). These were inspired by the threshold-crossing analysis. Each individually plausible, but empirically wrong — the CP did rise above 91%.

**41425 (Lisa Cook, 0.55→0.48, YES):** The re-retrodiction found Manifold at 6.5% (vs Metaculus 11%) and treated the gap as bearish. But Manifold prices reflect the underlying event, not Metaculus CP movement. The agent also found SCOTUS oral arguments were Jan 21 (after the Jan 15 measurement), flipping this from a positive to a negative signal. More information, worse outcome.

**40497 (PSOE vs PP, 0.35→0.40, NO):** The "prior near 50%" anchor pulled the agent from a correctly-confident 0.35 toward the 0.50 midpoint, away from the correct NO.

**Pattern:** The "prior should be near 50%" prescription anchors toward 50% regardless of direction. It helps when the agent is underconfident on YES, but hurts when the agent is correctly confident on NO. Net effect: noise.

### Prompt Change: Softened Meta-Prediction Guidance

Replaced the prescriptive "prior near 50%" with tool-first guidance:
- Emphasized get_cp_history as the most important tool for meta-predictions
- Added caution that cross-platform prices (Manifold/Polymarket) reflect the underlying event, not CP movement
- Kept the "Nothing Ever Happens" exclusion (correct)
- Removed the 50% anchor

### Prompt Change: Numeric CDF Width

Replaced "Set WIDE intervals — forecasters systematically underestimate uncertainty" with context-dependent guidance: tighter intervals when evidence is strong (analyst consensus, mean-reverting quantities), wider for genuinely volatile quantities.

**Rationale:** 50% CI coverage was 83% (target 50%). Outcomes consistently land near the agent's median. The blanket width instruction caused uniform overdispersion regardless of predictability.
