# Meta-Reflection: Post 41521 - UNGA Venezuela Resolution

## Executive Summary
Post 41521 asks whether the UNGA will adopt a resolution condemning a "US operation in Venezuela" before Feb 1, 2026. My final forecast is ~8% probability. The question references a specific event ("the" operation) but my research could not identify what it is. The extremely short timeframe (~20 days), historical rarity of UNGA condemning US operations, and US diplomatic leverage all point strongly toward NO.

## Evidence Assessment
**Strongest FOR (Yes):**
- The question exists with 96 forecasters, suggesting a real event occurred
- UNGA CAN act quickly (Russia-Ukraine ES-11/1 in 5 days)
- Emergency Special Sessions can be convened at any time
- Latin American solidarity could drive a resolution

**Strongest AGAINST (No):**
- UNGA has virtually never formally condemned US military operations (not Iraq, not Syria, not others)
- US has enormous diplomatic leverage to prevent/delay
- Very short timeframe (20 days)
- January is typically low-activity for UNGA
- No evidence found of any draft resolution or emergency session being called
- Even Russia-Ukraine (with near-universal condemnation) took several days

**Smart disagreer would say:** "If the US actually invaded Venezuela, the global outcry would be enormous and Latin American + developing nations would fast-track an emergency session." This is fair, but even so, getting from zero to adopted resolution in 20 days while the US actively opposes it is a steep challenge.

## Tool Audit
- web_search: Returned empty results across many query variations - this is a significant limitation
- search_exa: Also returned empty results
- get_cp_history: No data points (question too new or AIB limitation)
- polymarket: No matching markets
- get_coherence_links: No related questions found
- All tools functionally worked but returned empty results, suggesting either the events are too recent or search indexing hasn't caught up

## Subagent Decision
Used two researcher subagents in parallel - one for the specific event, one for UNGA mechanics/history. This was appropriate given the dual information needs. Could have also used an analyst for base rate calculation but the qualitative nature of the question didn't warrant it.

## Calibration
This is a predictive question. I'm forecasting 8%, which is directionally confident toward NO. Key data that would move me: (1) evidence an emergency session has already been called would move me to 25-40%, (2) evidence a draft resolution already has co-sponsors would move me to 40-60%, (3) evidence the operation never happened or is minor would move me to 2-3%.
