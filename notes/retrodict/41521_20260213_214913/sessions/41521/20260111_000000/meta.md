# Meta-Reflection: UNGA Resolution on US Venezuela Operation (Post 41521)

## Executive Summary
Post ID: 41521. Final forecast: ~22% YES. The question asks whether the UNGA will adopt a resolution condemning the US's January 3, 2026 military operation in Venezuela (Operation Absolute Resolve - bombing military bases, capturing President Maduro) before February 1, 2026.

## Evidence Assessment

**Strongest evidence FOR my forecast (supporting NO at ~78%):**
1. 8 days have passed since the attack with NO UNGA activity - no Emergency Special Session called, no resumed regular session. For comparable operations (Grenada, Panama), UNGA acted within 8-9 days while already in session. The absence of early UNGA action is informative.
2. The Iraq 2003 precedent - despite massive international opposition to the Iraq War, UNGA never adopted a condemnation resolution. This shows even highly controversial US operations can escape formal UNGA condemnation.
3. No precedent for an Emergency Special Session being convened specifically to condemn a US military operation. All previous ESS were for other P5 members (USSR, Russia) or regional conflicts.
4. The evolving diplomatic situation (negotiations, political prisoner releases, Rodriguez cooperating) may reduce urgency for UNGA condemnation.

**Strongest evidence AGAINST (supporting YES):**
1. Historical precedent: Both comparable unilateral US Western Hemisphere operations (Grenada 1983, Panama 1989) were condemned by UNGA within days. The base rate for this specific type of operation is 100%.
2. The operation is arguably the most provocative US action since Iraq 2003 - capturing a sitting head of state, bombing a sovereign nation's military infrastructure. International law experts universally condemn it.
3. Strong coalition of condemning countries (Brazil, China, Colombia, France, Mexico, Russia, Spain) with motivation and UNGA votes.
4. 20 days remain - procedurally enough time for ESS or resumed session.

**Smart disagreer argument:** The Grenada/Panama precedent combined with the sheer drama of the operation (capturing a head of state is unprecedented in modern international relations) should dominate the Iraq counter-precedent. The UNGA's 80th session can be resumed by the President with minimal procedural hurdles, and the pressure from Russia, China, and Latin America makes action highly likely within 20 days. The 8-day delay is explained by the January recess, not lack of political will.
